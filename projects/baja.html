Front Powertrain Guarding

Motorcats Baja SAE | Design & Manufacturing

Overview

Designed and manufactured protective guarding for the Baja SAE vehicle's front powertrain components to meet strict SAE competition safety requirements. The guard needed to shield the driver in case of a failure in the front joint of the Powertrain. This needed to be met while also minimizing weight for speed purposes and volume for ergonomic purposes.

Design Constraints

  • SAE Safety Compliance: Must meet rulebook specifications for protective guarding, including minimum coverage area and structural integrity requirements
  • Weight Budget: Limited mass while meeting other specifications
  • Durability: Must survive in case of a catastrophic failure of the powertrain and also people standing on it
  • Manufacturing: Producible with available shop equipment and within team budget

Design Process

Started with CAD modeling to determine optimal geometry that would protect the driver while minimizing frontal area and weight.

Material selection focused on aluminum alloy sheet metal for primary structure, balancing strength, weight, and formability. Selected 6061-T6 aluminum for its excellent strength-to-weight ratio and weldability.

Key Features

  • Powder-coated finish for corrosion resistance and team branding

Manufacturing

Fabricated using sheet metal brake forming.

Technologies & Skills

SolidWorks Sheet Metal Design Material Selection

Results

Successfully passed SAE technical inspection on first attempt. The guard protected the driver from the powertrain.
