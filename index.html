About Me

I'm a sophomore at Northwestern University doing a BS in Biomedical Engineering (3.93 GPA, Dean's List every quarter). My work currently sits on the more electrical/software side of engineering but I want to transition to more biomedical focused roles in the future.

As Software Lead for Northwestern's NASA Lunabotics team (NUSTARS), I architect ROS2-based autonomous navigation systems that must operate reliably in GPS-denied. As the rover is being currently developed and we are a first year team, I am working on a lot of the low-level systems including CAN protocol motor control, ROS2 integration and camera/Lidar sensor fusion.

My interest in medical robotics was shaped by an internship at Hypervision Surgical, a UK medical technology startup developing hyperspectral imaging for real-time surgical guidance. I'm drawn to smaller biomedical companies where I can contribute meaningfully to projects with a cross-disciplinary perspective.
